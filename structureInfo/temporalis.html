Temporalis is a fan shaped muscle overlying the lateral aspect of the skull within the temporal fossa medial to the zygomatic arch.

Contouring Specifics:
In patients with sarcopenia due to loss of skeletal muscle mass difficulties arise when identifying the cranial border.
In this circumstance contouring all visible temporalis muscle is recommended.

Function ▷
Elevates the mandible (jaw closure)
Posterior muscle fibres retract the mandible.

Origin ▷
Floor of temporal fossa and deep surface of temporal fascia.

Insertion ▷
Tendon inserts onto the coronoid process of the mandible extending to the most distal mandibular molar.

Innervation ▷
Mandibular branch of trigeminal nerve.

Blood supply ▷
Facial artery and maxillary artery.
